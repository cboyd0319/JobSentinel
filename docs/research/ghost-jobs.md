# Ghost-Jobs Research

This brief condenses research and guidance about stale postings, reposts,
evergreen roles, pipeline roles, closed jobs, and scam separation.

## Key signals

- Ghost-job risk can come from many causes: stale listings, already-filled
  roles, paused hiring, evergreen requisitions, pipeline collection, reposts, or
  aggregator lag.
- A company-site posting is stronger evidence than a third-party copy, but it is
  not proof of active hiring.
- Scam risk is different from ghost-job risk. Scam signals involve money
  requests, early sensitive-data requests, fake checks, suspicious domains, or
  unrealistic pay.
- Reposting, first-seen age, last-seen age, source mismatch, and vague role text
  can help users decide whether to verify before tailoring.

## Product implications

- Treat ghost-job detection as risk guidance, not a verdict on employer intent.
- Track first seen, last seen, repost count, closure signals, source quality, and
  user corrections.
- Prefer official company or ATS pages when available.
- Warn users before they spend serious time on stale or unverified postings.
- Keep scam warnings separate from stale-posting warnings.

## Open evaluation ideas

- Track how often high-risk roles later close, disappear, or repost.
- Compare user feedback against automated risk labels.
- Measure whether verify-before-tailoring guidance reduces wasted tailoring time.
- Review warning copy for accuracy and legal caution.
