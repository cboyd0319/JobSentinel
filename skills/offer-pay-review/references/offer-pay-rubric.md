# Offer Pay Rubric

Use this when comparing written offers, verbal claims, total compensation,
deadline pressure, or counter and decline drafts.

## Evidence Hierarchy

| Evidence | Weight |
| -------- | ------ |
| Written offer or contract | Strongest |
| Written recruiter email | Strong, but confirm final terms |
| Verbal recruiter or manager claim | Useful, but must be written before relying on it |
| Public market data | Context only; date, geography, level, and source matter |
| User assumption | Label as assumption |

## Total Compensation Checklist

- base pay
- bonus, commission, sign-on, equity, vesting, refreshers, cliffs, and exercise
  periods
- healthcare, retirement, PTO, leave, equipment, learning budget, relocation,
  parking, commute, travel, and remote/hybrid terms
- start date, review cycle, promotion path, overtime, expected hours, and
  repayment clauses

## Deadline Pressure

Flag:

- deadline not in writing
- deadline before documents are complete
- pressure to answer without benefits, relocation, or role scope details
- mismatch between verbal and written terms
- relocation, visa, tax, noncompete, or repayment complexity

## Counter Note Shape

1. Appreciation and role interest.
2. Specific ask.
3. Evidence: scope, market, competing written offer, level, commute, or total
   package gap.
4. Flexibility or alternate option.
5. Request for written confirmation.

## Decline Note Shape

1. Appreciation.
2. Clear decline.
3. Optional brief reason that does not over-share.
4. Professional close.

Do not invent competing offers, current pay, market data, legal claims, or
deadlines.
