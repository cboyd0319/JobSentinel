# Current Source Checks

Use this when the review relies on market data, pay transparency, salary-history
rules, noncompete or repayment terms, relocation or tax complexity, visa-related
offer terms, or any jurisdiction-specific claim.

## Source Hierarchy

Prefer current, dated, primary sources:

1. Written offer, contract, benefits packet, or recruiter email from the employer.
2. Official government or regulator pages for laws, forms, or jurisdiction rules.
3. Employer-published role range or compensation policy.
4. Reputable salary or market data with date, geography, level, and role match.
5. User assumptions or anecdotes, labeled as assumptions.

## Freshness Rules

- Verify volatile legal, tax, visa, pay-transparency, salary-history,
  noncompete, and benefits claims from current official sources before relying
  on them.
- Do not present salary websites, H1B data, crowdsourced reports, or old job
  posts as complete market evidence.
- Keep geography, job level, employment type, schedule, and total compensation
  scope attached to every benchmark.
- If source dates are missing or stale, label the comparison as directional.

## Legal And Financial Boundaries

- Flag contract, repayment, noncompete, visa, tax, or relocation terms for a
  qualified professional when they affect the decision.
- Do not give legal, tax, immigration, or investment advice.
- Do not tell the user what they must disclose about current pay, protected
  status, family circumstances, medical information, or private constraints.

## Output Note

When current-source checks are needed, separate:

- confirmed written facts;
- current external evidence and source date;
- assumptions;
- questions to ask before accepting, countering, or declining.
