# Weekly Review Rubric

Use this when replanning from a tracker, stopping weak lanes, or deciding where
the next week of job-search effort belongs.

## Review Inputs

- Tracker rows grouped by lane, source, role family, and status.
- Applications, screens, interviews, referrals, skips, and no-response items.
- User constraints that changed: location, schedule, salary floor, start date,
  commute, authorization, travel, or urgency.
- Resume variants and outreach variants used during the week.
- Energy, confidence, and fatigue signals that should change pacing without
  lowering the quality bar.

## Lane Decision Rubric

| Signal | Continue | Adjust | Stop or pause |
| ------ | -------- | ------ | ------------- |
| Evidence fit | Resume has strong proof for most core requirements | Proof exists but title language or level is off | Core requirements remain unsupported |
| Source quality | Active employer or ATS pages, warm contacts, current posts | Mixed boards, duplicates, or thin details | Mostly stale, scraped, weak, or no-contact roles |
| Response quality | Replies, screens, recruiter context, or referrals | Saves but few useful contacts | No meaningful responses after focused effort |
| Constraint fit | Pay, schedule, location, and must-haves work | One open constraint needs verification | Repeated pay, commute, authorization, or schedule mismatch |
| Effort value | Tailoring time is proportional to opportunity | Requires narrower filters or better templates | High effort with low confidence or poor fit |

## Weekly Review Steps

1. Count outcomes by source and lane: saved, applied, screen, interview, offer,
   no response, rejected, skipped, and withdrawn.
2. Name the top two sources by quality, not volume.
3. Name the weakest lane or source and the evidence behind that decision.
4. Check pacing: reduce low-fit cold applications, add recovery time, or shift
   toward referrals/direct sources when fatigue is hurting judgment.
5. Decide one change: narrow titles, broaden adjacent roles, switch sources,
   increase outreach, reduce low-fit applications, or update the resume base.
6. Set next week's actions with owners, dates, and stop rules.

## Output

End with:

- keep doing
- change next week
- stop or pause
- user must confirm
- first three actions for tomorrow
