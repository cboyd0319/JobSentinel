# Outreach Channel Patterns

Use this when selecting message length, cadence, and channel-specific structure.
The user reviews and sends all messages manually.

## Email

- Best for referrals, recruiter replies, longer context, or attachments after
  review.
- Keep under 160 words unless the user asks for a longer note.
- Include subject, exact role link or title, one evidence sentence, one ask, and
  a low-pressure close.

## LinkedIn Message

- Best for light context, recruiter replies, alumni, warm contacts, or asking
  whether email is better.
- Keep under 90 words for first contact.
- Do not automate account activity, collect cookies, or imply a relationship
  that does not exist.

## Text

- Use only for real contacts where texting is normal.
- Keep it conversational and short.
- Avoid resumes, salary floors, sensitive details, and large asks.

## Recruiter Reply

Include:

- thanks
- role interest or mismatch
- 2-3 fit signals
- location, schedule, authorization, or pay constraints only when intentional
- next question or availability

## Referral Ask

Use when a real connection or credible weak tie exists:

1. Context: how the user knows them.
2. Role: exact company and role.
3. Fit: one truthful evidence sentence.
4. Ask: referral, perspective, or right contact.
5. Easy out: no pressure.

## Follow-Up Cadence

- After no response: wait 5-7 business days for warm outreach.
- After interview: send thank-you within 24 hours.
- After recruiter process timeline passes: follow up after timeline plus 1-2
  business days.
- Pause when there is no new reason to write.
