# Interview Story Quality Rubric

Use this when building or improving interview stories for recruiter,
behavioral, panel, work-sample, portfolio, case, or technical interviews.

## Story Fields

- situation
- task or goal
- action the candidate personally took
- result or observable outcome
- reflection or lesson
- evidence source
- role requirements it supports
- sensitive details to remove

## Quality Rubric

| Level | Indicators | Action |
| ----- | ---------- | ------ |
| Strong | Specific context, clear personal action, outcome, tradeoff, and role mapping | Use as a primary story |
| Usable | True story with clear action but weak outcome or missing scale | Tighten and ask for detail |
| Thin | Generic, team-only, no clear action, or no result | Rebuild from evidence |
| Risky | Confidential, inflated, unsupported, or contradicts resume/application | Do not use until corrected |

## Format Branches

Recruiter:

- 60-second pitch, target role, logistics, pay range if intentional, and 2-3 fit
  signals.

Behavioral:

- 5-8 stories covering conflict, ambiguity, ownership, learning, collaboration,
  quality, and impact.

Panel:

- Repeat core themes without sounding scripted. Prepare one question per
  interviewer role when known.

Work sample, case, or portfolio:

- Clarify scope, assumptions, time box, data rights, deliverables, and what not
  to include.

## Debrief Prompts

- What questions were asked?
- Which answers were strong?
- Which answers need a cleaner story?
- What did the employer reveal about pay, timeline, scope, schedule, or risk?
- What is the next action and date?
